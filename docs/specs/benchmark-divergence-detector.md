# Benchmark-Divergence Detector — real grades vs benchmark predictions

**Origin:** operator, topic 11960, 2026-07-12: *"our current benchmark assumes some models perform very well in certain scenarios; if the real-life data suggests otherwise then we need to re-evaluate all aspects of that scenario, including whether the model is getting enough context, whether the model has an appropriate prompt, and whether the benchmark is appropriately representing the scenario."* Operator approved building the loop ("yes, please proceed") and gave two first-class requirements (R1, R2 below).

**This spec = Increment A** of the benchmark-feedback loop: the divergence DETECTOR. Observe-only, in-repo. Increment B (bench prompt-parity faithfulness, ACT-1195) and Increment C (representative-cases feed) are **explicitly non-scope** (see Non-scope). <!-- tracked: ACT-1195 --> The A build proceeds identically regardless of the operator's pending A-only vs A+B scope steer — that choice sequences B, it never blocks A.

**Parent standard:** *Decision Provenance & Outcome Review* — whose rule text explicitly includes "graded real cases feeding its bench battery"; this detector is the review-the-outcomes-against-the-promise layer. Dual lineage acknowledged: the compounding-improvement signal it produces is squarely *Never-Waste Feedback — corrections compound*.

**Dependency status (honest):** the LLM-Decision Quality Meter (`docs/specs/llm-decision-quality-meter.md`) is **operator-approved with PR #1458 OPEN, auto-merge armed on green CI — NOT yet merged**. This spec's build has a HARD ORDERING CONSTRAINT: it extends the meter's grading chokepoint and rollup schema, which have no target until #1458 merges. The detector cannot build/merge before the meter lands. Live *function* is additionally gated on the meter's `provenance.uniformSeam` being flipped live and grades accumulating.

## Problem statement

The **benchmark** (INSTAR-Bench v2) is a PREDICTION: per (task, model), a battery-run pass-rate asserting "model M is good at scenario S." The **quality meter** is GROUND TRUTH: per decision-point, the real production grade outcomes (right/wrong/unknown). Today nothing compares the two, so when reality contradicts the prediction — the signal the operator wants to act on — it is invisible; and the grade data ages out on retention clocks before anything learns from it. This feature compares real grade-rates to predicted rates per (decision-point × model) and guarantees the comparison consumes the data before retention deletes it (R1), across every machine (R2).

## Data flow — THREE distinct stages (the conceptual backbone)

1. **RECORD (continuous, per-decision):** the meter's correlation spine + provenance. Every enrolled high-stakes decision records what it saw + chose. No benchmark involvement.
2. **GRADE (periodic, evidence-triggered):** the meter's grade-pass stamps each recorded decision right/wrong/unknown as evidence matures, with its OWN cursor (`decision_grading_cursor`). **Increment A extends this chokepoint:** at grade time, the pass ALSO upserts per-`(decisionPointId, model, day)` right/wrong/unknown INTEGER counts into the (schema-extended) `decision_quality_rollup` — the durable, content-free essence, written long before any raw-row retention clock fires.
3. **ANALYZE (periodic, less frequent — THIS feature):** the divergence detector reads **CLOSED days from the rollup** (never raw rows), pool-collects every machine's rollup aggregates (R2), derives the real grade-rate per (decision-point × model), compares to the mirrored benchmark passRate, and upserts findings carrying the ranked questions. It has its OWN per-machine day-watermark (R1), distinct from the grading cursor.

The improvement fork: grades = ground truth → a divergence routes to **fix-the-SYSTEM** (context/prompt) vs **fix-the-BENCHMARK** (drift/unrepresentative). The detector points; humans and later increments fix.

## Frontloaded decisions

- **FD1 — Prediction source (in-repo mirror), grounded against the live batteries.** The "prediction" is the battery-run **pass-rate**: INSTAR-Bench's `aggregate.mjs` computes `passRate = passes / deterministic` per `(taskId, model)` (each case carries an `expected` answer). It is **per-model** (`ab-run.mjs` A/B-runs tasks across models). The detector runs in-repo against a small, content-free, git-tracked mirror: `{ taskId → { perModel: { <batteryModelId> → passRate }, benchedPromptSource, promptFidelity, benchedPromptHash, capturedAt } }` — `benchedPromptSource` is the task's `source` (e.g. `src/core/CompletionEvaluator.ts:137`), `benchedPromptHash` is sha256 over the task's resolved `promptTemplate` **normalized as: exact template string, LF line endings, no trim** (the ONE pinned canonicalization both the off-repo pull and the in-repo live hasher use). Heavy battery runs stay off-repo; mirror population is an operational pull step (see FD9 for staleness handling). Posture: **unified** — a git-tracked file, identical on every checkout by construction.
- **FD2 — Comparison unit and arithmetic (per decision-point × model, settled-grades denominator).** For each `decisionPointId` with BOTH a `LLM_BENCH_COVERAGE {task}` entry AND rollup rows: `realGradeRate = right / (right + wrong)` over **SETTLED grades only** — unknown/expired are EXCLUDED from the rate and reported separately as `unknownShare` (apples-to-apples with the battery's `passes/deterministic`, whose denominator also excludes non-deterministic cases). The sample gate is **graded-n = right + wrong ≥ minSample** (grades, never decisions-made). Additional honesty bounds: `unknownShare` above `maxUnknownShare` (default 0.5) ⇒ `insufficient-evidence` (the grade stream is too unsettled to compare); the meter's per-point orphan-outcome share above `maxOrphanShare` (default 0.10) ⇒ verdict downgraded to `partial` with flag `orphanTainted: true` (the meter's deferred cross-machine outcome routing, ACT-1199, systematically biases mobile decision-points toward unknown — an all-machines-online incompleteness distinct from an offline peer). <!-- tracked: ACT-1199 -->
- **FD3 — Divergence test (deterministic, CI-aware).** A comparison flags only when `|realGradeRate − predictedRate(model)| > max(divergenceThreshold, wilsonHalfWidth95(realGradeRate, gradedN))` — the Wilson-interval half-width guard keeps sample noise at the floor from flipping verdicts (at n=20 a rate near 0.65 has ±~0.21 CI, wider than the 0.15 threshold). Every finding carries `gradedN`, `unknownShare`, and `ciHalfWidth` so a reader can weight it. Deterministic formula, no new dependencies.
- **FD4 — Verdict enum (frozen, direction-split, precondition-first).** `verdict ∈ { divergent-worse, divergent-better, aligned, insufficient-evidence, no-benched-baseline, precondition-failed, partial }`, with `preconditionReason ∈ { prompt-drifted, hash-unverifiable, stale-mirror }` set iff `precondition-failed`:
  - **Q0 gates everything:** `benchedPromptHash ≠ live prompt hash` ⇒ `precondition-failed / prompt-drifted` — the divergence is a benchmark bug, routed to Increment B, NEVER a model verdict (suppresses divergent/aligned in BOTH directions; a false `aligned` on a drifted benchmark is as wrong as a false `divergent`). Live hash uncomputable for the pair ⇒ `precondition-failed / hash-unverifiable` — "unverifiable" is NEVER treated as "faithful." Mirror older than `mirrorStalenessMaxDays`, or the live prompt hash changed after `capturedAt` ⇒ `precondition-failed / stale-mirror` (staleness SUPPRESSES actionable verdicts; it never merely annotates one).
  - **Direction split:** `divergent-worse` (real < predicted) carries the ranked questions 1–3; `divergent-better` (real > predicted) carries a DIFFERENT first question — "is this grade-rate inflated? (evidence rule too lenient / recurrence-suppressible / unknown-excluded denominator)" — plus the meter's evidence-strength breakdown reference, because over-performance is exactly the signature of a gamed or too-lenient grade stream and must never read as "promote this model."
  - `no-benched-baseline`: the production model (after FD5 normalization) has no battery entry — surfaced (deduped per distinct normalized model), itself a signal the battery should add that model; a normalization MISS is distinguished from a genuinely-unbenched model in the finding detail (`unmapped: true|false`) so a mapping bug can never masquerade as a coverage signal.
- **FD5 — Model-id normalization (fail-closed, versioned, in Increment A).** A versioned, in-repo, exact-match table maps production model ids (as the meter records them) ↔ battery model ids. **Fuzzy/substring matching is forbidden.** An unmapped production id ⇒ `no-benched-baseline (unmapped: true)` — never a silent join to a foreign baseline. The table ships in A (seeded for the two first meter customers; a near-empty table degrades every row to `no-benched-baseline`, the safe inert default — populating it is data work, not schema work).
- **FD6 — Live prompt hash source (pinned).** For each enrolled comparison pair, the LIVE hash is **recomputed by the detector from the in-repo prompt builder** the task's `benchedPromptSource` names, under the same canonicalization as FD1 (the two wave-1 customers — the external-hog classifier and the completion judge — have in-repo prompt builders). Increment A computes the Q0 faithfulness verdict itself (it is not merely surfaced text). A pair whose live prompt is not statically resolvable ⇒ `hash-unverifiable` (FD4).
- **FD7 — R1: analyzed watermark + retention safety (rollup-based — NO coupling to the meter's raw-row prune).** The detector **never reads raw decision_quality/outcome rows and never gates their prune** — the meter's existing retention is byte-identical untouched (a dark or stalled detector cannot grow or starve the meter's store; the round-1 hazard family is dissolved structurally, not bounded). R1 is honored one layer up: the grade essence is rolled into the durable content-free rollup **at grade time** (stage 2, long before any raw clock), and the ANALYZE stage consumes **closed days** (day < today UTC — counts for an open day still mutate) from the rollup, tracked by a per-machine watermark keyed `(machineId, decisionPointId, model, analyzedThroughDay)`. Exactly-once per closed day; idempotent re-runs are no-op upserts. If a machine's watermark ever lags within `unanalyzedLossWarnDays` (default 7) of the rollup's own retention edge, the analysis records a content-free `unanalyzedLoss` counter on the next finding — loss is counted, never silent. Raw-row pruning stays entirely the meter's business; a wiring test asserts the detector performs ZERO writes and ZERO prune-gating against raw tables.
- **FD8 — R2: cross-machine collection at analysis time (local-first, pool-for-verdict-only).** Each machine's rollup is machine-local (inheriting the meter's grades posture). The ANALYZE stage runs on **exactly one machine — the serving-lease holder** (no per-machine duplicate findings; a non-holder's cadence tick is a no-op). It pool-collects every machine's closed-day rollup aggregates (tiny, content-free) via a bounded, parallel, peer-URL-guarded fan-out under one deadline. An offline/unreachable machine ⇒ that pair's verdict is `partial` (with `coverage: {machinesReporting, machinesKnown}` metadata), re-collected when it returns — the detector NEVER concludes from an incomplete pool, and **a missing peer NEVER stalls any machine's watermark or the meter's retention** (verdict-only coupling). Chronic non-conclusion is surfaced: `partial`/`insufficient-evidence` for `chronicCycles ≥ K` (default 3) consecutive analyses sets `chronic: true` naming why (which machine persistently offline at cadence / graded-n stuck) — a reassuring non-conclusion is not allowed to hide a real divergence indefinitely; the analysis time carries jitter so a fixed sleep schedule cannot systematically exclude a machine.
- **FD9 — Peer + mirror inputs are untrusted (clamped, bounded, attributed).** Pool-collected peer aggregates pass a strict type-clamped allowlist (counts finite non-negative integers, ids charset/length-clamped `^[A-Za-z0-9._/-]{1,128}$`, enums clamped, unknown fields dropped); an implausible aggregate (rate outside [0,1], counts exceeding plausible volume) is a classified `pool.suspect` row — excluded and surfaced, never silently merged; every finding records per-machine contribution counts so a poisoned input is attributable and reversible. Mirror fields pass the same clamps at load; all queries parameterized. taskId/model/decisionPointId are untrusted data everywhere.
- **FD10 — Output surfaces (observe-only, frozen envelope).** `GET /benchmark-divergence` (Bearer; 503 dark) returns `{ enabled, dryRun, mirror: { capturedAt, staleDays, stale }, findings: [FINDING], summary: { byVerdict }, scope }` where `FINDING = { taskId, decisionPointId, model, verdict, preconditionReason?, realGradeRate, predictedRate, delta, gradedN, unknownShare, ciHalfWidth, orphanTainted, chronic, coverage, unmapped?, benchedPromptHash, mirrorCapturedAt, analysisWindow: {fromDay, toDay}, firstSeenAt, lastSeenAt, advisory: true, questions: [static ranked question TEXT per FD4] }`. **Content-free guarantee:** the surface never joins to or inlines the meter's raw decision context/prompts — question 1's "inspect what the wrong decisions saw" is answered by POINTERS (correlation-id ranges) into the meter's own surfaces, within their own retention/auth; a test asserts no payload field appears. `?scope=pool` merges peers' findings through the FD9 allowlist, fronted by the shared PoolPollCache (TTL, load-shed-to-stale), newest-`lastSeenAt`-wins per key — advisory data, divergence tolerable and flagged. `POST /benchmark-divergence/analyze` (Bearer, rate-limited, single-flight, idempotent) triggers one pass. **`advisory: true` is stamped on every finding row**: no consumer may gate/route/escalate on a finding except as a SIGNAL into a proper authority (Signal vs. Authority; the future model-routing consumer must pass through its own gate).
- **FD11 — History without unbounded growth.** Findings are upserted per `(taskId, decisionPointId, model)` for the latest view AND appended as content-free history rows keyed `+ (analysisWindow.toDay, mirrorCapturedAt)` so a benchmark re-run or prompt change never silently overwrites the divergence record; history is capped per key (`maxHistoryPerKey`, default 50, oldest pruned) — bounded by construction.
- **FD12 — Execution + engineering pins.** The ANALYZE pass runs in-process on the server's shared FeatureMetricsLedger connection (the meter's grade-pass execution model — serialized, no SQLITE_BUSY class), with `busy_timeout` 5000ms defense-in-depth. Reading the rollup for closed days is a single grouped query per pass (`GROUP BY decisionPointId, model, day`), bounded by `maxDaysPerAnalysis` (default 35 closed days per pass) — O(rollup rows in window), never O(raw rows), never a per-correlation-id loop. The driving job is a built-in declarative template `benchmark-divergence-analysis` shipping `enabled: false`, `supervision: tier0` (justification: fully deterministic aggregation + comparison — no LLM step exists in the pass), wired through BOTH `installBuiltinJobs` (fresh installs) and `migrateBuiltinJobs` (existing agents) per Migration Parity.
- **FD13 — Ship posture.** Dark on the fleet, live-in-dryRun on a development agent (`benchmarkDivergence.enabled` omitted ⇒ dev-agent gate; explicit false = off). **dryRun (default true) = ZERO durable writes** — no findings, no watermark advance, no history rows; would-analyze summaries are logged content-free. Because analysis is rollup-based, a frozen watermark has zero retention consequence (FD7). Routes 503 when dark. Rollback = flag off; the additive tables sit inert; the rollup schema extension is additive columns with defaults (no destructive migration).

`enabled` (dev-gate when omitted) · `dryRun` (true) · `divergenceThreshold` (0.15) · `minSampleForRates` (falls through to `provenance.quality.minSampleForRates`, 20) · `maxUnknownShare` (0.5) · `maxOrphanShare` (0.10) · `analysisCadenceHours` (24, jittered) · `maxDaysPerAnalysis` (35) · `mirrorPath` (`src/data/benchmarkPredictions.json`) · `mirrorStalenessMaxDays` (30) · `unanalyzedLossWarnDays` (7) · `chronicCycles` (3) · `maxHistoryPerKey` (50).

## Durable schema (additive)

- `decision_quality_rollup` — EXTENDED to key `(decision_point, model, day)` carrying `right_n, wrong_n, unknown_n` INTEGER counts (rates derived at read; integer counts are prune-safe and mergeable; a single float rate is neither). Written at the meter's grading chokepoint (stage 2). Additive migration; existing rows keep a model default of `''` (pre-extension aggregate, excluded from comparisons).
- `benchmark_divergence_findings` — latest view + capped history (FD11), all fields content-free (FD10), `advisory: true` stamped.
- `benchmark_analysis_watermark` — `(machineId, decisionPointId, model, analyzedThroughDay, updatedAt)` (FD7). Terminology: `decisionPointId` everywhere; "component" is not a key in this feature.

**Backup/restore:** all three are content-free and ride the meter's SQLite DB backup — included, NOT added to any at-rest exclusion list (unlike the content-carrying JP log). A backup→restore round-trip preserving a finding + rollup essence is asserted in tests.

|---|---|---|
| Divergence verdict (FD3/FD4) | **invariant** | Deterministic threshold + Wilson formula + enumerable predicates over clamped inputs; conservative defaults (`insufficient-evidence`/`partial`/`precondition-failed` on any uncertainty); observe-only (`advisory: true`). **Caveat: invariant ONLY while observe-only** — any future increment that alerts/gates/routes on a verdict flips this to judgment-candidate and owes that design (incl. Standard-B for an alert). |
| Q0 faithfulness (FD6) | **invariant** | Hash equality under one pinned canonicalization; uncomputable ⇒ `hash-unverifiable`, never assumed faithful. |
| Model-id normalization (FD5) | **invariant** | Fixed exact-match lookup; miss ⇒ `no-benched-baseline (unmapped)` fail-closed; fuzzy forbidden. |
| Peer-aggregate admission (FD9) | **invariant** | Deterministic type/range clamps; implausible ⇒ excluded + surfaced (`pool.suspect`). |
| Analyzer election (FD8) | **pass-through** | Reuses the existing serving-lease machinery; this spec adds no election logic. |

## Multi-machine posture

- Rollup + watermark: **machine-local** — `machine-local-justification: operator-ratified-exception` — inherits the quality meter's ratified machine-local grades posture (meter spec §FD10/§5.5, operator-approved 2026-07-12 topic 11960; PR https://github.com/JKHeadley/instar/pull/1458 — the machine-verifiable ref; this marker is valid only once #1458 is merged, which the build-ordering constraint already requires).
- Findings: **written on the lease-holder, proxied-on-read** (`?scope=pool` merge, FD10) — one authoritative writer per pool-wide computation (FD8), merged read tolerant of holder churn.
- Mirror: **unified** (git-tracked in-repo file — identical on every checkout by construction; staleness is fleet-identical).
- No user-facing notices (no one-voice surface needed); no generated URLs; nothing strands on topic transfer (state is not topic-keyed).

## The ranked questions (per flagged finding)

`divergent-worse`: 0-precondition (already enforced as FD4 verdicts) → 1 enough context? (pointers into the meter's provenance for the wrong decisions) → 2 right prompt? (live vs benched) → 3 does the battery represent reality? (→ Increment C).
`divergent-better`: 1 is the grade-rate inflated? (lenient evidence rule / recurrence-suppressible / denominator) → 2 is the battery too hard / unrepresentative? → 3 genuine improvement (only after 1–2 are excluded).

## Testing (all three tiers, per Testing Integrity)

- **Unit:** FD3 both sides of `max(threshold, CI)` + boundary at graded-n floor; unknownShare/orphanShare bounds ⇒ insufficient-evidence/partial; FD4 enum incl. drifted-hash ⇒ `precondition-failed` on BOTH sides of the threshold (never divergent OR aligned); stale-mirror suppression; unmapped-model fail-closed (never joins a foreign baseline); watermark exactly-once per closed day (no skip/double; open day never consumed); dryRun = zero durable writes (findings, watermark, history all frozen); Wilson half-width math; history cap prune. Injected clocks throughout.
- **Wiring integrity:** the detector performs ZERO raw-table writes and ZERO prune-gating (meter retention byte-identical with detector dark, dryRun, AND live); rollup essence written at grade time before any raw prune (ordering assertion); `installBuiltinJobs` + `migrateBuiltinJobs` both carry the analysis job; mirror staleness actually computed from `capturedAt`.
- **Cross-machine:** pool-collect merges N machines' aggregates; offline machine ⇒ `partial` + re-collect + `chronic` after K; **local watermark advances with ALL peers offline** (verdict-only coupling); non-lease-holder tick is a no-op (no duplicate findings when two machines run the cadence); peer clamp rejects out-of-range aggregates as `pool.suspect`; `?scope=pool` field-allowlist strips hostile fields.
- **Integration:** GET 200-with-data / 503-dark / Bearer / envelope shape frozen (FD10) / no raw-context field ever present; POST rate-limit + single-flight + idempotent replay.
- **E2E (feature-alive):** single-machine boot, flag on ⇒ route 200-not-503.
- **Backup:** round-trip preserves finding + rollup.
