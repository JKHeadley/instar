## What Changed

Delivered commitments near the end of a large commitment store are now reconciled promptly instead of waiting five minutes for every 64-record slice. The existing bounded reconciler drains successive slices across event-loop turns, then resumes its normal five-minute cadence after a complete sweep.

The existing completion trend now includes an additive live cumulative series. Its final row represents the current partial UTC day, so a genuinely delivered commitment visibly increments `windowTotal` and `currentDayCount` immediately. The established direction and ratio still use complete days only.

## What to Tell Your User

The throughput reading now moves when a real registered deliverable is marked delivered, including during the current day. It remains an observation only and does not rank people, choose work, impose targets, or gate anything.

## Summary of New Capabilities

- Prompt bounded recovery of completion rows in mature commitment stores.
- Live window, current-day, and cumulative completion counts on the existing trend route.
- Strict pool validation for live cumulative arithmetic and consecutive UTC dates.
- Idempotent delivery accounting through the existing commitment and lifecycle ledger path.

## Evidence

Unit coverage proves the trend rises from zero to one and then two after real commitment deliveries. Integration coverage places a delivered commitment beyond the first 64-record reconciliation slice and proves it appears promptly and remains idempotent after restart. E2E coverage reads the live cumulative count through the authenticated real-server route.
