## What Changed

The benchmark-feedback loop's **Increment A** ships: a divergence detector that compares the quality meter's REAL production grades (per decision-point × model) against INSTAR-Bench's PREDICTED pass-rates, surfacing observe-only findings with the operator's ranked re-evaluation questions (more context? better prompt? bigger model? is the benchmark itself wrong?). A `decision_quality_rollup_by_model` table rides the meter's existing transaction (recompute-from-raw, supersede-correct, freeze-don't-zero beyond the raw retention floor); a serving-lease-holder-only analyzer pool-collects every machine's matured-window aggregates (nothing missed across machines — R2) and guarantees grade data is consumed before retention deletes it, with any un-analyzed loss counted visibly (R1); findings carry Wilson-interval-honest verdicts, chronic-streak honesty (a stale mirror or offline peer cannot hide forever), and FD9-hardened pool merging (peer free-text never crosses — questions are regenerated locally; all inputs type/volume-clamped with `pool.suspect` classification). Ships dark + dryRun (`benchmarkDivergence.*`), observe-only (`advisory: true` on every finding), fleet job manifest `enabled:false`. The benchmark mirror ships absent on non-bench machines by design (loud `precondition-failed`, never silent); the refresh runbook is tracked as ACT-615.

## What to Tell Your User

None user-visible today — the feature ships dark and observe-only. When your operator later enables it, the agent gains an honest answer to "are the models actually performing the way our benchmark predicted?" — grounded in real production outcomes, with plain-language questions when reality and prediction diverge. Nothing about how the agent behaves, routes, or messages changes; findings are advisory reading material.

## Summary of New Capabilities

Internal (dark until enabled): `GET /benchmark-divergence` (+ `?scope=pool`), `POST /benchmark-divergence/analyze` (lease-gated), `GET /benchmark-divergence/rollup-aggregates` (peer collection); the `benchmark-divergence-analysis` cadence job (fleet-dark); per-(decision-point × model) grade rollups feeding the future benchmark-reevaluation loop.
