# Claim Verification v1

## What changed

Instar now observes factual claims in completed assistant turns through a bounded, Claude-only extraction lane. The observer is dark: it records counterfactual assessments and never delays, blocks, rewrites, or sends a message. Existing Action-Claim routing remains the sole outbound authority.

The v1 verifier supports same-turn tool evidence plus fresh exact snapshots for current sessions, commitments, and registered guards. Capacity and pull-request claims deliberately remain unverifiable because v1 has no canonical oracle for them. Criticality floors are deterministic and cannot be lowered by model confidence or hedging.

## What to tell your user

Claim Verification is gathering privacy-scrubbed, metadata-only evidence about factual reliability in the background. It does not change what gets sent. The dashboard labels its counts as server-admitted observations so queue drops and unavailable providers are not mistaken for complete coverage.

## New capabilities

- Bounded all-response observation with per-topic fairness, queue expiry, and local cost ceilings.
- Strict four-claim envelope validation with byte-accurate source spans and protected-cue gap accounting.
- Scrubbed, pseudonymous local audit and benchmark rows excluded from backup and file serving.
- Privacy-qualified pool aggregates with minimum row/topic thresholds and explicit partial results.
- Bounded legacy-log quarantine and retention receipts.

## Evidence

The focused v1 unit suite covers scrub-before-provider handling, schema refusal, criticality floors, supported and unsupported verifier adapters, protected-cue gaps, metadata-only persistence, file/backup exclusions, hook migration, and unchanged legacy Action-Claim behavior. Build and full-suite evidence is recorded in the accompanying side-effects artifact and Instar development trace.
