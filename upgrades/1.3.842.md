## What Changed

Mentor prompts can now reach a mentee on another machine through signed, recipient-bound MeshRpc delivery into the existing A2A inbox. The receiving machine binds the authenticated sender machine to the configured mentor before the existing marker, role, bot-ID, and target-agent checks run. Telegram remains a post-acceptance visible mirror only.

## What to Tell Your User

A mentor and mentee can now run on different machines without relying on impossible Telegram bot-to-bot delivery. The prompt is delivered over Instar's authenticated machine channel and appears in the Telegram mentor topic for supervision only after the mentee accepts it. A visible Telegram post by itself never counts as delivery.

## Summary of New Capabilities

- Signed cross-machine mentor-to-mentee A2A delivery
- Sender-machine-to-mentor identity binding at the recipient
- Existing target, role, marker, bot-ID, and replay defenses preserved
- Honest failure semantics: no sent ledger/outstanding state from a mirror alone

## Evidence

- Unit coverage for signed registered-peer admission, mesh success, and mirror-only refusal
- Integration coverage for sender-machine binding and downstream inbox refusal
- Real AgentServer E2E coverage across a simulated machine seam into the mentee hook
- Regression note: Telegram bots do not receive other bots' messages; Telegram is observability, never the delivery authority
